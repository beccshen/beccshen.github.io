Rebecca Shen is an aspiring landscape architect whose broad interests lie in the synthetic relationships of landscapes in the era of rapid social, ecological, and political shifts due to climate change. Rebecca is navigating her challenging role as a designer in this context. Specifically, she is curious about how designers can create landscapes to foster interspecies (particularly with nonhuman animals) living communities, sustainable local food production, and community-engaged, smaller-scaled applications of climate change mitigation.

Rebecca is pursuing a Master in Landscape Architecture at Harvard Graduate School of Design, where she will be graduating in Spring 2023. She graduated from Washington University in St. Louis in 2020, earning a Bachelor of Science in Architecture magna cum laude with minors in Computer Science and Landscape Architecture. Currently, Rebecca is a landscape designer at Terrain Work, a landscape architecture, urban design, and public art studio in New York City.




During her free time, you will probably find Rebecca painting figures and places with watercolors or oils, attempting new jump rope tricks, or hiking the beautiful surroundings of her home in Upstate New York.
